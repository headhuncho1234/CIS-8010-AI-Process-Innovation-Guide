One-Page Cram Sheet — The Highest-Yield 20

Read this twice the morning of the exam. If you only have 20 minutes, you have this.

Gimpel's Universal Themes

  • You don't get paid for what you do. You get paid for the value you create. REPEATED
  • Automating tasks DOES NOT change the process. REPEATED
  • A business = a collection of processes + the resources that make them possible.
  • Problem-to-Be-Solved (PTBS): the elemental core reason the service is valuable to the consumer.
  • Outside-in thinking focuses on solving customer problems instead of internal operations.

BPMN Must-Memorize

  • 3 basic shapes: Activities = rounded rectangle, Events = circle, Gateways = diamond.
  • Task vs. Event: You assign tasks. Events just happen.
  • Event borders: Start = thin, Intermediate = double, End = thick.
  • Throwing vs. Catching: Throwing is filled; catching is not filled.
  • Messages only between pools. A process cannot jump pools.

Lean Six Sigma Must-Memorize

  • Lean: maximize customer value while minimizing waste.
  • Waste test: if the customer will not pay for it, it is waste.
  • DOWNTIME: Defects, Overproduction, Waiting, Non-value-added processing, Transportation, Inventory, Motion, Employees underutilized.
  • DMAIC: Define, Measure, Analyze, Improve, Control.
  • Lean reduces waste; Six Sigma reduces variability.

AI & Digital Must-Memorize

  • RPA is not AI. Rules engines are preprogrammed.
  • Intelligent Automation = RPA + decision-making AI.
  • Agentic AI: AI can act autonomously toward goals.
  • RAG: LLM uses external data beyond original training data.
  • First-Mover Advantage is a myth. TRAP
Class 1 Problem-to-be-Solved, Business Processes & BPMN

1.1 Problem-to-be-Solved (PTBS)

PTBS: The elemental core reason the service is valuable to the consumer of the service.
RepeatedYou don't get paid for what you do. You get paid for the value you create.

1.2 Business Processes

Business: A business is a collection of processes and the resources that make those processes possible.
RepeatedImproving processes will improve a business. Transforming processes will transform a business.

1.3 BPMN

A given BPMN diagram should have one and only one interpretation.
ShapeDefinition
ActivityA step in the process. Represents work/action performed.
EventA signal that something has happened.
GatewayControls flow branching, merging, and parallel actions.
TrapMessages flow only between pools. A process cannot jump across pools.

2.1 Lean Core Concept

Lean: Maximize customer value while minimizing waste.

Waste: Anything other than the minimum amount of resources absolutely essential to add value.

Class 3 Digital BPM, RPA, AI, GANs & RAG

3.1 BPM

BPM: A discipline that discovers, models, analyzes, measures, improves, and optimizes business processes.

3.2 RPA

RPA: Use of bots or call activities to perform automated tasks.
TrapRules engines are not AI. Automating tasks does not change the process.

3.3 AI

AI: Machine behavior and function that exhibits the intelligence and behavior of humans.

3.4 Generative AI and RAG

Generative AI: Trainable algorithms that create new content.
RAG: Retrieval-Augmented Generation lets an LLM reference additional data beyond its training set.
Class 4 Agentic AI & Intelligent Process Automation

4.1 Intelligent Automation

Intelligent Automation: RPA with decision-making AI.

4.2 Agentic AI

Agentic AI: An AI system designed to understand complex workflows and achieve goals autonomously with little to no human intervention.

4.3 Agentic Characteristics

CharacteristicDefinition
AutonomyChooses steps to pursue goals without human supervision.
Adaptive learningLearns from interactions and updates strategies over time.
Workflow optimizationOptimizes workflows and business processes.
